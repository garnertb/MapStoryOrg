Host a Channel

Channels let you and your colleagues share collections of StoryLayers and MapStories into one customized url at MapStory.org. They’re great for businesses, nonprofits and academic institutions that generate lots of data and want a single place to share these data and MapStories with the world.

Explore channels | Launch a channel now

Launch an Initiative

Some MapStory projects are so large, and so fundamental to the mission of “improving our understanding of the rich past, complex present and uncertain future we share” that the MapStory Foundation and various sponsors support them as Initiatives. For example, the MapStory: Borders & Annexations Initiative, sponsored by the Sunlight Foundation and kickstarted by Karl Phillips, maps municipal border changes throughout U.S. history. Ideas for Initiatives come from MapStorytellers themselves, so step forward and propose the next Initiative you’d like to see, or even better, to lead yourself.

Explore initiatives | Launch an initiative now

Share your skills

MapStory is a global community of storytellers from different walks of life. However, although everyone has their own story to tell, not all users have the data and cartography background to create StoryLayers and MapStories by themselves. The Volunteer Technical Community (VTC) steps into to share time, knowledge and talent to help others. It is composed of GIS experts and tech-savvy individuals who are committed to the task of helping aspiring non-technical MapStorytellers create their narratives.

Learn about the VTC | Join the VTC now

Become an Editor

Every MapStoryteller is an editor to some degree. Individual MapStorytellers have ultimate responsibility to edit their own work, and make it as excellent and accurate as possible. And any registered MapStory user also has the power to “peer review” the work of others by flagging errors, making comments, and rating content as you would a restaurant on Yelp. But a smaller number of MapStorytellers take on increased responsibility as official editors. Editors monitor content for compliance with MapStory values, respond to error flags, identify gaps in content that need to be filled, and more. Becoming an editor is a great way for MapStorytellers with fewer technical skills but a strong interest in content and quality to take leadership.

Learn more about editing MapStory | Become an editor now

Found a Chapter

MapStory is a global effort that also highly values local leadership and participation. Although the U.S. based nonprofit MapStory Foundation provides overall leadership for the project, independent chapters located around the world help support and promote MapStory. In some cases, chapters operate their own MapStory web instances or spin off related projects and initiatives.

Learn more about chapters | Found a chapter now

Organize an Event or Training

The internet is a powerful force for learning and collaboration. But nothing is better than getting together with neighbors and colleagues face-to-face. MapStory events are self-organized by MapStorytellers and might involve teaching MapStory skills, collecting data, writing code, showing off completed projects, raising funds, and more. We leave the work of leading your or event to you. But we’re happy to help you succeed by sharing resources, expertise and even some MapStory swag if we have it!

Read about past events and trainings | Find materials to help you plan

Code for MapStory

The MapStory platform is sustained and improved by a community of open source developers. Together, they establish a technical roadmap, build features, collaborate on designs and respond to ideas and request from the global user community. If you are a developer and want to earn your way into the MapStory developer community, you should check out Github, join the developers google group, and begin to speak out with your ideas and abilities.

Find us on Github | Join the Developers Google Group
